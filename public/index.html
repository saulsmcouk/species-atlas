The Woodlark's Comeback

Once reduced to fewer than 200 pairs in the 1980s, the woodlark has staged a remarkable recovery. This gentle heathland specialist, with its delicate song that seems to float from the sky, has benefited greatly from heathland restoration projects across southern England.

The data tells a story of hope: decade-by-decade growth, expanding breeding range, and a species stepping back from the brink. Yet challenges remain — woodlarks depend on actively managed heathland, making continued conservation efforts vital.
